<Section icon={Shield} title="Biomes and Base Spot Priorities">
Genesis is not a “wander anywhere” map. The biomes are built to punish lazy routing, and that makes base placement more important than raw loot hunting. If you want a safer start, choose a location that matches your first tame, your travel method, and your hazard tolerance.

<Callout type="tip" title="Base Spot Rule">
Pick the biome that supports your next two goals, not the one that looks the prettiest on a screenshot.
</Callout>

| Biome | Core Hazard | Best For | Base Spot Fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bog | Dense swamp pressure, canopy threats | Mobility tames, early scouting | Low if you need safety |
| Ocean | Deep water, waves, whirlpools | Sea travel, mobile bases | High for ocean teams |
| Arctic | Cold, steep terrain, avalanche risk | Strong kits, cold-ready tames | Moderate after prep |
| Volcanic | Heat, eruptions, fire danger | Endgame power routes | Low for new players |
| Lunar | Low gravity, alien terrain | Movement testing, advanced routes | Low until geared |

| Base Goal | Best Biome Choice | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Safe first camp | Ocean edge or mild transition zone | Better movement options and easier retreat lines |
| Resource staging | Bog or Arctic border | Good access, but still risky |
| Mobile tribe base | Ocean | Platforms and sea travel support moving operations |
| Late-game push | Volcanic or Lunar | Higher risk, higher reward routing |

| Biome | Featured Creatures | Practical Value |
|---|---|---|
| Bog | Bloodstalker | Fast traversal and canopy control |
| Ocean | Palaeoctopus, Megachelon | Sea utility and mobile ocean bases |
| Arctic | Ferox | Combat support and flexibility |
| Volcanic | Magmasaur | Forge-like industrial value |
| Lunar | Astrocetus | High-mobility fantasy and tribe play |

The ocean deserves special attention because the Ascended version expands it into a map-wide system with dynamic waves, buoyancy, islands, and hidden fortresses. That makes sea play more than a side activity. It can become your safest logistics lane if you build around it correctly.

<Callout type="info" title="Ocean Priority">
Ocean routes are strongest when you can move supplies, defend a platform, and retreat without getting trapped on land chokepoints.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Missions, Glitches, and Hexagon Progression">
Genesis progression is mission-first. HLN-A acts like your control center, and the mission loop gives the map structure. If you ignore missions, you end up undergeared and underprepared for the harsher biomes. If you lean into them early, the game starts to feel much more manageable.

<Callout type="info" title="Early Progress Loop">
Use missions to earn Hexagons, use Hexagons to buy useful supplies, then use those supplies to survive harder zones.
</Callout>
