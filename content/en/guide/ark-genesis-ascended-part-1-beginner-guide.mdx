<Section icon={Swords} title="Build a Practical Early Loadout">

Your first gear plan should solve three problems: movement, survival, and resource collection. Genesis Ascended Part 1 gives you a lot of flashy toys, but beginners get more value from tools and tames that help them reach the next mission safely.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Early Overcommitment">
Do not sink all your momentum into one expensive combat idea. A stable route, a useful tame, and a reliable resource tool are usually better first investments.
</Callout>

**Early priority loadout**

| Item or tame | Priority | Main use | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bloodstalker | High | Mobility and scouting | Great for traversal-heavy play and dangerous routes |
| Palaeoctopus | High | Ocean control and utility | Strong for sea travel and tribe support |
| Megachelon | High | Mobile ocean base | Gives ocean players a stable foundation |
| Magmasaur | Medium | Combat and forging support | Useful when you need industrial power and fire-themed progression |
| Hover Skiff | Medium | Transport and logistics | Helps with movement, base support, and tribe operations |

That list is not about raw power alone. It is about reducing the number of times you get stuck, lost, or exposed while trying to complete missions. Bloodstalker and Palaeoctopus stand out because they solve movement problems early. Megachelon becomes valuable if you want an ocean base style. Magmasaur helps once your crafting and combat loops start to mature. Hover Skiff is best when your tribe needs logistics more than frontline damage.
