<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Biomes for a Safe Start">

The biome layout is the main reason this guide matters. Genesis Ascended Part 1 is built around pressure zones, so the right first area saves time and resources.

<Callout type="info" title="Biome Priority">
Start where the terrain teaches you the game without punishing every mistake. Bog and Ocean are the most useful early learning spaces; the other regions are better after you have gear, tames, and a mission rhythm.
</Callout>

| Biome | Difficulty | Bring This | Early Value |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bog | Medium | Mobility, antidote-like caution, scouting tame | Good for traversal practice |
| Ocean | Medium to High | Water safety, range, transport plan | Strong for exploration and base routing |
| Arctic | High | Warmth, armor, discipline | Better later |
| Volcanic | Very High | Heat protection, heavy prep | Late-game feel |
| Lunar | Very High | Movement control, situational awareness | Advanced route |

The Ocean biome deserves special attention because the Ascended version pushes the water space much harder than a simple coastal zone. Dynamic waves, buoyancy, islands, and hidden fortresses make it a real route-planning layer, not just a body of water.

**Recommended first-biome order**
1. Bog edge or safer entry route
2. Ocean scouting pass
3. Mission return and reward spending
4. Arctic or Volcanic only after gear improves
5. Lunar when movement and combat are already stable

<Callout type="tip" title="Travel Tip">
If a biome forces you to fight the environment and the enemy at the same time, delay the visit until your inventory and tame lineup can absorb the loss.
</Callout>
