<Section icon={Shield} title="Biomes and Route Planning">

Genesis works because each biome pushes a different skill test. The map is not just scenery; it is a set of survival checks. Your route choice should match your gear, tame plan, and tolerance for risk.

<Callout type="info" title="Route Planning Rule">
Start with the biome that matches your weakest point. If you struggle with movement, prioritize mobility-heavy zones. If you struggle with combat, stay near mission-friendly paths first.
</Callout>

| Biome | Core Threat | Best Early Mindset |
|---|---|---|
| Bog | Swamp danger, canopy pressure | Move carefully and stay mobile |
| Ocean | Depth, waves, hidden hazards | Prep for travel and visibility |
| Arctic | Cold, peaks, avalanches | Carry survival support |
| Volcanic | Heat and eruption risk | Bring durable gear |
| Lunar | Low gravity, alien terrain | Plan movement before combat |

The Ocean deserves special attention. Ascended-style ocean design pushes dynamic waves, buoyancy, islands, and hidden fortresses into a much larger travel puzzle. That means your first ocean run should be about learning space, not forcing a fight.

| Ocean Feature | What It Changes | Player Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Dynamic waves | Unstable sea movement | Harder navigation |
| Buoyancy | Water travel feels different | Better boat planning |
| Islands | Break up open-water routes | More staging points |
| Hidden fortresses | Encourage exploration | Better reward hunting |

<Callout type="success" title="Strong Early Habit">
Mark one safe staging point per biome. That single habit cuts down on repeated deaths and helps you recover faster after bad encounters.
</Callout>

If your first impression comes from wandering blindly, the map can feel punishing. If you plan around one biome at a time, the same map starts to feel deliberate.

</Section>
