<Section icon={Map} title="Map Biomes, Hazards, and Ocean Overhaul">

The map side of Genesis Ascended Part 1 is built around pressure. Each biome pushes a different survival skill, and the ocean layer deserves special attention because it is not just a travel lane anymore.

<Callout type="warning" title="Biome Warning">
Do not treat the ocean as a side zone. It adds dynamic waves, buoyancy, hidden spaces, and long-range threat management that can punish weak preparation.
</Callout>

| Biome | Main Risk | Best First Goal | Strong Utility |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bog | Swamp pressure, canopy threats | Learn fast movement routes | Bloodstalker |
| Ocean | Depth, waves, travel risk | Build a safe sea plan | Megachelon, Palaeoctopus |
| Arctic | Cold and avalanche danger | Bring insulation and pace your travel | Ferox |
| Volcanic | Heat and eruption hazards | Enter only with a survival plan | Magmasaur |
| Lunar | Low gravity, alien terrain | Practice movement control | Astrocetus |

The practical rule is simple: pick the biome that matches your gear, not the biome that looks coolest on the map screen. A lot of players fail because they test their ego instead of the hazard profile.

| Biome Priority | What It Teaches | Early Takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bog | Mobility under pressure | Good for traversal practice |
| Ocean | Base planning and survival spacing | Strong once your sea kit exists |
| Arctic | Temperature control | Punishes careless routing |
| Volcanic | Fire management | Better after you have durable tools |
| Lunar | Movement discipline | Rewarding, but unstable for new runs |

<Callout type="info" title="Map Mindset">
Use the map as a route planner. The best path is usually the one that lets you keep moving, keep your resources stable, and keep your first tame alive.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Missions, Hexagons, and the Starter Route">

Genesis progression runs through HLN-A missions, and that matters because mission structure gives you direction before the map starts trying to eat you. The safest early play is to build routine, earn rewards, and only then move into harsher content.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open HLN-A and pick an easy mission">
    Start with manageable objectives so you can learn the mission flow without losing your kit to avoidable mistakes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear glitches while you move">
    Treat glitches as bonus progress. They keep your route efficient and help you stack extra value on the same trip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spend Hexagons on bottlenecks">
    Use rewards on resources and useful progression items instead of hoarding everything for a later problem you have not solved yet.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Scale into tougher biome runs">
    Once your mission rhythm is stable, push deeper into harder zones with a clearer loadout and a better tame plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Mission Type | What It Rewards | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Enemy wave missions | Combat practice | Builds confidence and reward flow |
| Escort missions | Control and protection | Teaches route discipline |
| Boss tracking | Progression pacing | Keeps late-game goals visible |
| Dino Races | Movement and handling | Good for map familiarity |
| Glitch cleanup | Extra progression value | Makes each run more efficient |

<Callout type="success" title="Starter Loop">
If you feel stuck, go back to easier missions and clean up your reward economy first. Hexagons are most useful when they remove friction from your next run.
</Callout>
